Kannur - A Coastal Haven Awaiting Your Arrival

Kannur, also known as Cannanore, is a beautiful coastal city located in the northern part of Kerala. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, historical significance, and natural beauty. Kannur is a popular destination for tourists seeking a blend of relaxation and adventure. From beaches to backwaters, from historical forts to traditional art forms, Kannur has something to offer for every kind of traveler.The main attraction of Kannur is its beautiful and pristine beaches.They offer a serene environment and stunning views of the sea. Visitors can engage in various activities like swimming, sunbathing, and beach sports.Kannur is also famous for its traditional art forms. Theyyam, a popular ritualistic art form, is unique to the region. The vibrant costumes and mesmerizing dance performances make it a must-see attraction. Other cultural activities like folk songs and dance forms also attract tourists.Kannur is a perfect blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. With its scenic beaches, historical landmarks, traditional art forms, and adventure options, Kannur is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Kerala.

Explore the Top Tourist Destinations in Kannur

Payyambalam Beach

Payyambalam Beach, situated at a distance of 2 km from Kannur town, is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. The beach is famous for its long stretch of golden sands and calm waters, making it ideal for swimming and sunbathing. The picturesque view of the sunset over the Arabian Sea is a sight to behold. The beach also has a well-maintained park and a children's play area, making it a perfect spot for families. Several food stalls and small shops selling souvenirs and handicrafts are located along the beach. The beach is well-maintained and offers various amenities such as lifeguards, changing rooms, and food stalls. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll along the promenade or enjoy the fresh sea breeze while savoring local delicacies. The beach is easily accessible and remains a favorite among tourists visiting Kannur.

St. Angelo Fort

St. Angelo Fort, also known as Kannur Fort, is a majestic historical monument located in the Kannur district of Kerala. The fort was built by the first Portuguese Viceroy of India, Don Francisco de Almeida, in the year 1505. The fort has played a significant role in the history of Kannur, witnessing several battles and invasions. Today, it stands as a remarkable symbol of the Portuguese influence on Indian architecture. The fort is a popular tourist attraction in Kannur, known for its impressive architecture and stunning views of the Arabian Sea. Visitors can explore the fort's various chambers, tunnels, and bastions and learn about the fort's history and significance from the information boards placed around the fort.The fort's well-preserved structure and historical significance make it a must-visit attraction in the region.

Muzhappilangad Beach

Nestled on the coast of Kannur, Muzhupilangad Beach is the longest drive-in beach in India, extending up to 4 km. This pristine beach is an ideal destination for those seeking tranquility away from the bustling city life. The soft golden sand and the azure waters of the Arabian Sea make for a stunning view that is bound to take your breath away. The gentle waves of the sea make it a perfect spot for swimming, sunbathing, and long walks along the shore. You can also indulge in adventurous activities like beach volleyball, parasailing, and jet-skiing. The beach is lined with several shacks offering delicious seafood and refreshing drinks. A visit to Muzhupilangad Beach is an absolute must for any beach lover.

Arakkal Museum

Located in the heart of Kannur, the Arakkal Museum is a fascinating destination for history buffs and culture enthusiasts. Once the palace of the Arakkal dynasty, the museum showcases a diverse collection of artifacts that provide a glimpse into the royal legacy of the region. From weapons and armor to furniture and jewelry, the exhibits offer a unique insight into the rich cultural heritage of Kannur. Visitors can also explore the various galleries and learn about the history of the Arakkal dynasty and its influence on the development of the region.Arakkal Museum is a must-visit destination for history buffs and those interested in learning about the cultural heritage of Kerala. The museum is open on all days except Mondays and public holidays, and the entry fee is nominal.
