Discover the Untouched Beauty of Malappuram

Malappuram, located in the northern part of Kerala, is a land of abundant natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. The district is famous for its lush green forests, scenic hills, serene backwaters, and stunning waterfalls. Malappuram is also home to several historical monuments and temples, which showcase the vibrant culture and traditions of the region. One of the main attractions of Malappuram is the Kottakkunnu park, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and the nearby Thirunavaya river. The district is also home to several other stunning natural wonders, including the Adyanpara Waterfalls, Keralamkundu Waterfalls, and the Nedumkayam Rainforest. Another must-visit tourist spot in Malappuram is the Thirunavaya Navamukunda Temple, which is located on the banks of the Bharathapuzha river. The temple is famous for its exquisite architecture and intricate carvings, and is believed to be one of the oldest temples in the region.Malappuram is a perfect destination for those who want to experience the beauty and culture of Kerala in a serene and peaceful setting.

Explore the Top Tourist Destinations in Malappuram

Adyanpara Waterfalls

Adyanpara Waterfalls is a picturesque natural wonder located in the Malappuram district of Kerala. The waterfall cascades from a height of around 300 feet and is surrounded by lush greenery, making it a beautiful spot for picnics and relaxation. The waterfalls are also ideal for trekking and hiking, with a trek path leading to the top of the waterfall where visitors can get a stunning panoramic view of the surrounding landscape.The best time to visit Adyanpara Waterfalls is during the monsoon season, when the waterfall is in full flow and the surrounding forests are lush and vibrant. Don't forget to bring your camera to capture the breathtaking views and memories of your visit to Adyanpara Waterfalls.Adyanpara Waterfalls is truly a hidden gem in the lap of nature that is not to be missed while exploring the beauty of Malappuram.

Padinharekara Beach

Padinharekara Beach is a scenic coastal gem located in Malappuram district of Kerala, known for its serene atmosphere and picturesque beauty. It is a perfect destination for those who want to spend some peaceful moments away from the hustle and bustle of the city. The long stretch of golden sand and the cool sea breeze make it an ideal spot for a leisurely stroll or sunbathing. The beach is surrounded by lush green coconut groves and is a popular spot for fishing. Visitors can indulge in activities such as swimming, boating and beach volleyball. The nearby lighthouse offers a panoramic view of the beach and the surrounding area. A visit to Padinharekara Beach is a must for anyone visiting Malappuram.Padinharekara Beach is also famous for offering breathtaking views of the confluence of the Bharathapuzha River and the Arabian Sea.

Kottakkunnu

Kottakkunu is a popular hill station located in Malappuram district of Kerala. It is situated at an altitude of 1600 feet above sea level and is surrounded by lush greenery, tea plantations, and beautiful valleys. The hilltop offers panoramic views of the surrounding hills and the city of Malappuram. One of the main attractions of Kottakkunnu is the Kottakkunnu Fort, which was built by the Zamorin of Kozhikode in ancient times. The fort has now been converted into a beautiful park and is a popular picnic spot for families and tourists. The park also has a children's play area, a musical fountain, and a large open-air theater that hosts cultural events and programs. Kottakkunnu is a perfect place for nature lovers and those seeking a peaceful retreat away from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Biyyam Kayal Park

Biyam Kayal Park is a popular eco-tourism destination located in Malappuram, Kerala. The park is known for its serene natural beauty and is a favorite spot among nature lovers and birdwatchers. The park is situated near the Biyam Kayal Lake and covers an area of around 10 acres. It features a variety of flora and fauna, including over 60 species of birds, butterflies, and other insects. Visitors can enjoy boating, trekking, and picnicking in the park's peaceful surroundings. The park also has a children's play area, making it an ideal destination for families. A visit to Biyam Kayal Park is a must-do for anyone seeking to explore the natural beauty of Malappuram. The park offers a unique opportunity to connect with nature and rejuvenate one's mind and body.
