Escape to the Untouched Beauty of Wayanad: A Nature Lover's Paradise

Wayanad, also known as the "Green Paradise", is a popular hill station located in the Western Ghats of Kerala. The district is surrounded by misty hills, lush green forests, and a rich variety of flora and fauna. Wayanad is a perfect destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and wildlife enthusiasts. The district is known for its pristine natural beauty and is a perfect getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life. Wayanad has several attractions for tourists, such as waterfalls, wildlife sanctuaries, historical sites, and trekking trails. The district is home to some of the most picturesque waterfalls in Kerala, such as Meenmutty Waterfalls, Soochipara Waterfalls, and Kanthanpara Waterfalls. The wildlife sanctuaries in Wayanad, such as Muthanga Wildlife Sanctuary and Tholpetty Wildlife Sanctuary, are home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including elephants, tigers, leopards, and various species of birds. Wayanad is also known for its rich cultural heritage and historical sites, such as Edakkal Caves, which showcase ancient rock art and engravings. The district also has several trekking trails, such as the Chembra Peak trek, which offers panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys. Overall, Wayanad is a perfect destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and those seeking a peaceful getaway in the lap of nature.

Explore the Top Tourist Destinations in Wayanad

Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary

Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts and wildlife lovers. Located in the Wayanad district of Kerala, this sanctuary is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. Spread over an area of 344 square kilometers, the sanctuary boasts of rich biodiversity, with several rare and endangered species of plants and animals found here. The sanctuary is known for its population of Asiatic elephants, which can be spotted wandering through the forest.Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary is a must-visit destination for nature and wildlife enthusiasts.

Edakkal Caves

Edakkal Caves are a unique prehistoric site located in the Wayanad district of Kerala. The caves are nestled amidst lush green forests and offer a glimpse into the lives of the Neolithic era. The caves are known for their ancient petroglyphs or rock carvings, which are believed to date back to 6000 BC. The trek to the caves is a scenic one, with panoramic views of the Western Ghats along the way. Visitors can explore the two main caves and see the intricate carvings that depict human and animal figures, tools, and symbols. A visit to Edakkal Caves is a must for history buffs and nature lovers alike.

Banasura Sagar Dam

Banasura Sagar Dam is one of the most visited tourist spots in Wayanad. It is the largest earthen dam in India and the second-largest in Asia. The dam is built across the Karamanathodu River and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and forests. Visitors can indulge in various activities like boating, trekking, and nature walks in the nearby areas. The lush greenery and the tranquil environment of the place make it an ideal destination for those seeking peace and rejuvenation amidst nature. Don't miss the chance to witness the mesmerizing sunset from the top of the dam.

Pookode Lake

Pookode Lake is a beautiful freshwater lake located in the Wayanad district of Kerala. It is surrounded by lush green hills and dense forests, making it a popular tourist destination for nature lovers. The lake is approximately 40 meters deep and covers an area of 8.5 hectares. Pookode Lake is surrounded by a nature park, which is home to a variety of wildlife such as peacocks, deer, and even elephants. The park is also a popular spot for bird watching, with several species of birds frequenting the area. Pookode Lake is a must-visit destination for those seeking a tranquil escape amidst the beauty of nature.
