Unveiling the Hidden Beauty of Kerala - Kasaragod

Escape to the Serenity of Northern Kerala's Best Kept Secret - Kasaragod. Embrace the unspoiled beauty of this district, which offers a perfect blend of culture, nature, and tranquility. Explore the historical landmarks and ancient temples that hold tales of a bygone era. Soak up the sun on the pristine sandy beaches or take a leisurely cruise along the placid backwaters. Kasaragod is truly a hidden gem waiting to be discovered.Apart from its natural beauty and cultural heritage, Kasaragod also offers visitors a chance to indulge in its unique cuisine. The district is known for its delicious seafood, which is a must-try for any food enthusiast. The local cuisine is a blend of various influences, including the traditional Malabari cuisine and the coastal cuisine of Karnataka. The district is also famous for its handloom industry, which produces high-quality traditional sarees and other textiles.Kasaragod is a perfect destination for those looking for an offbeat vacation. The district's natural beauty, cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine offer a unique experience that is hard to find anywhere else. Whether you are a history buff, a foodie, or an adventure seeker, Kasaragod has something to offer for everyone.

Explore the Top Tourist Destinations in Kasaragod

Bekal Fort

Bekal Fort is an ancient fort located in Kasaragod district of Kerala, India. It is a well-preserved fort that dates back to the 17th century and is spread over an area of 40 acres. The fort offers a stunning view of the Arabian Sea from its strategic location on a cliff. It was built by the Nayakas of Ikkeri and later was taken over by the Vijayanagara Empire. The fort has been witness to several battles and is a prime example of the military architecture of the time.The fort offers stunning views of the sea and is a great spot for photography. A visit to Bekal Fort is a must for history buffs and those who wish to marvel at the architectural wonders of the past.

Chandragiri Fort

Chandragiri Fort, located in Kasaragod district of Kerala, is a historical monument that attracts tourists with its rich heritage and stunning architecture. Built in the 17th century by Sivappa Nayak of the Ikkeri dynasty, the fort is known for its majestic structure and beautiful views of the surrounding landscape. It is situated on a hilltop and provides panoramic views of the Arabian Sea and the nearby towns. The fort is also home to the Chandragiri Fort Mosque and a temple dedicated to Goddess Kali, which reflects the religious harmony of the region. A visit to this fort is a must for those interested in history and architecture.

Kappil Beach

Kappil Beach is a pristine white sand beach situated at a distance of about 6 km from the Bekal Fort. It is a perfect place to relax and unwind amidst the serene surroundings. The beach is lined with coconut trees, providing a picturesque view of the Arabian Sea. You can take a stroll along the beach or simply laze around on the sandy shores. Adventure enthusiasts can indulge in water sports like parasailing and jet skiing. The beach is also known for its beautiful sunset views, making it an ideal spot for nature lovers and photographers.

Ranipuram

Ranipuram, also known as the "Ooty of Kerala," is a beautiful hill station located in the Kasaragod district of Kerala. The hill station is situated at an altitude of 750 meters above sea level and is a perfect getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life. Ranipuram is well-known for its natural beauty, lush green forests, and breathtaking views of the Western Ghats. Trekking is a popular activity here, and tourists can enjoy several trekking trails that lead to stunning viewpoints, waterfalls, and streams
